# Copilot Context - Battery Charger Project
# Created: 2026-04-13

## Hardware Summary
- ESP32 with ADS1115 at 0x48, GAIN_FOUR (+-1.024V)
- 3x LiFePO4 batteries in parallel, ~48-55V nominal
- 5x PSUs controlled via PWM
- Charger output: up to ~17A per battery = ~50A total on main cables
- GTI (afterburner) for discharge
- Voltage divider on charger output (NOT battery terminals) feeds ADS channel 0

## Current Problem - Battery Voltage Reading
The ADS reads the charger output side of the wiring, not the battery terminals directly.
The main cables from charger to connector block carry ~50A total and have significant
resistance, causing ADS to read higher than actual battery voltage under load.

### Original wiring (before fix):
- + from charger: thick cable (approx 4 AWG) - runs cool
- - from charger: 8 AWG - gets HOT at 50A (at/over rating for continuous use)
- 8 AWG fits through WCS1700 toroid, 4 AWG does not

### Analysis:
- Error grows with current (~0.21 V/A) up to ~12A then plateaus at ~2.7-3.0V
- Plateau is due to wiring resistance increasing with temperature (cables get hot at 15A+)
- The -ve cable (8 AWG) is the bottleneck - all 50A returns through it, it gets hot
- The +ve cable is already thick (4 AWG) and runs cool
- Current used in battery.cpp correction is charger.Irms (per-battery AC measurement) - WRONG
  - Should be total DC output current (~50A), with correct sign (+ charging, - discharging)
- The connector block is also a suspect for contact resistance at high current
- NOTE: per-battery current from BMS bluetooth on one battery only - others may not share equally

## Planned Hardware Fix - Cable Upgrade + WCS1700 Current Sensor

### Cable upgrade plan:
- Replace both + and - main cables with 2 x 8 AWG in parallel on each
- 2 x 8 AWG in parallel ~ equivalent to 4 AWG, rated ~80A combined - heat problem solved
- Both cables same length so current shares equally (1mm difference = negligible imbalance)
- At each end: stuff both cable ends into a single connector and fill with solder
  - Tin each cable individually first
  - Use a big iron or gas torch - small iron won't have enough thermal mass for 2 x 8 AWG
  - Heat the connector, not the solder
  - Use plenty of flux

### *** IMPORTANT - fit WCS1700 on one cable BEFORE soldering ***
- Thread one of the +ve 8 AWG cables through the WCS1700 toroid BEFORE soldering the ends
- If you solder first you cannot get the toroid on afterwards - solid ring, no gap
- WCS1700 on one +ve cable measures ~25A (half the total)
- Multiply reading by 2 in software to get total current

### WCS1700 wiring:
- WCS1700 VCC  -> 3.3V
- WCS1700 GND  -> GND
- WCS1700 Aout -> ADS1115 channel 1 (A1)
- WCS1700 Dout -> leave unconnected (or ESP32 GPIO for overcurrent alert later)
- Note cable direction through toroid - determines sign of output, verify at test time

### WCS1700 on 3.3V supply:
- 0A midpoint: 1.65V (Vcc/2)
- Sensitivity: ~23mV/A (scaled from 35mV/A at 5V)
- At 25A (half of 50A total): output = 1.65 + (25 * 0.023) = 2.225V - well within GAIN_ONE
- Use ADS GAIN_ONE (+-4.096V) for channel 1 when reading current
- Switch back to GAIN_FOUR for channel 0 (battery voltage) after each current reading
- Multiply raw current reading by 2 to get total charger output current
