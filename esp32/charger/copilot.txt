# Copilot Context - Battery Charger Project
# Created: 2026-04-13

## Hardware Summary
- ESP32 with ADS1115 at 0x48, GAIN_FOUR (±1.024V)
- 3x LiFePO4 batteries in parallel, ~48-55V nominal
- 5x PSUs controlled via PWM
- Charger output: up to ~17A per battery = ~50A total on main cables
- GTI (afterburner) for discharge
- Voltage divider on charger output (NOT battery terminals) feeds ADS channel 0

## Current Problem - Battery Voltage Reading
The ADS reads the charger output side of the wiring, not the battery terminals directly.
The two main 8 AWG cables (~1m) from charger to connector block carry ~50A total and
have significant resistance, causing ADS to read higher than actual battery voltage under load.

### Analysis:
- Error grows with current (~0.21 V/A) up to ~12A then plateaus at ~2.7-3.0V
- Plateau is due to wiring resistance increasing with temperature (cables get hot at 15A+)
- The 2 x 8 AWG main cables carry ~50A total (3 batteries in parallel)
- 8 AWG is at/over its rating for 50A continuous - cables should be upgraded to 4-6 AWG
- Current used in battery.cpp correction is charger.Irms (per-battery AC measurement) - WRONG
  - Should be total DC output current (~50A), with correct sign (+ charging, - discharging)
- The connector block is also a suspect for contact resistance at high current
- NOTE: per-battery current from BMS bluetooth on one battery only - others may not share equally

## Planned Fix - WCS1700 Current Sensor
Hardware available: WCS1700 (+-70A, bidirectional hall effect sensor module)
- Has both Dout (digital overcurrent) and Aout (analog proportional) outputs
- Solid toroid ring - main cable must be disconnected to thread through

### Wiring:
- WCS1700 VCC  -> 3.3V
- WCS1700 GND  -> GND
- WCS1700 Aout -> ADS1115 channel 1 (A1)
- WCS1700 Dout -> leave unconnected (or ESP32 GPIO for overcurrent alert later)
- Thread POSITIVE main cable from charger output through the toroid (single pass)
- Note cable direction - determines sign of output, verify at test time

### WCS1700 on 3.3V supply:
- 0A midpoint: 1.65V (Vcc/2)
- Sensitivity: ~23mV/A (scaled from 35mV/A at 5V)
- Use ADS GAIN_ONE (+-4.096V) for channel 1 when reading current
- Switch back to GAIN_FOUR for channel 0 (battery voltage) after each current reading
