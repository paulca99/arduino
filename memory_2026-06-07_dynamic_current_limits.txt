- New safety/control direction implemented for testing:
  - Pylontech CAN charge/discharge current limits should be dynamic rather than fixed.
  - GBLI 5001-style per-battery current limit is treated as **40 A per fresh usable battery**.
  - With 1 fresh usable battery, CAN current limit should be about **40 A**.
  - With 2 fresh usable batteries in parallel, CAN current limit should be about **80 A**.
  - If one enabled battery becomes stale/offline/not contributing, current limit should drop rather than allowing two-pack current from one pack.
- Important refinement agreed:
  - current limit scaling should use batteries that are **enabled + fresh + contributing**, not merely configured/enabled.
  - A battery should not count toward charge current if charge MOS is off, data is stale, or it is not contributing.
  - A battery should not count toward discharge current if discharge MOS is off, data is stale, or it is not contributing.
- Cell-voltage safety policy agreed for Boston Swing 5300 Li-ion 14S packs:
  - use weakest/strongest cell voltage, not pack average, as the safety authority.
  - low-cell discharge derating idea:
    - min cell >= 3.25 V: full discharge current
    - 3.20-3.25 V: reduced discharge current
    - 3.15-3.20 V: strongly limited discharge current, around 10 A total
    - below about 3.15 V: stop discharge by advertising zero discharge current / discharge not allowed
  - high-cell charge derating idea:
    - max cell < 4.05 V: full charge current
    - 4.05-4.10 V: reduced charge current
    - 4.10-4.15 V: strongly limited charge current, around 10 A total
    - >= about 4.15 V: stop charge by advertising zero charge current / charge not allowed
